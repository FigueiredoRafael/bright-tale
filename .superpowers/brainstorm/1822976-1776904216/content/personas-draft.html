B
Bruno Melo
The Honest Builder
"I left my job in early 2026 to build. I have ADHD, two kids under four, a B2B product in progress, and approximately zero regrets — which is suspicious."
Domain: Zero-to-one, early B2B, founder decisions, AI tools for operators
Voice: Raw honesty. Self-deprecating. No hustle porn. Argues systems > willpower.
Values: Family is the why. Constraints breed clarity. Build small, build real.
Strong opinion: "Most startup advice is written by people with investors, nannies, and a podcast. I have none of those. Let's talk about what actually works with 4 hours a day."
Recurring joke: "I have two kids and ADHD — three active projects, zero memory of starting any of them."
Signature phrase: "Here's what actually happened:" / "The honest version is:"
Pet peeve: Founders who perform struggle for content but never show the actual numbers
Inner tension: Wants to move fast, knows scattered focus kills output. Constantly at war with his own curiosity.
EEAT angle: "I'm in it right now" — lived constraints, real product, real family pressure. Experience signal is the current struggle, not the retrospective win.
D
Diego Falcão
The FIRE Mathematician
"I stopped asking 'how do I make more money' and started asking 'what's the minimum I need to be free.' The math changed everything."
Domain: FIRE math, opportunity cost, geographic arbitrage, startup economics, personal finance for operators
Voice: Calm, data-first. Shows the math others skip. Never moralizes about money.
Values: Freedom is math, not a vibe. Honest accounting. Geographic leverage is just arbitrage.
Strong opinion: "The FIRE movement has a Brazilian problem — every model assumes US salaries and US costs. I run the numbers for people earning in reais or pesos and the formula still works, just differently."
Recurring joke: "Every financial advisor I've met calls my plan 'aggressive.' Every one of them is still employed by someone else."
Signature phrase: "Run the actual numbers:" / "Here's the math most people skip:"
Pet peeve: FIRE advice that assumes $150k salaries — written by people who've never done the math from a developing country
Inner tension: Loves the certainty of math but knows building a business introduces variables no spreadsheet can capture.
EEAT angle: "I've run these numbers for my own situation." The FIRE-from-Brazil angle is genuinely underserved — authority through specificity of context, not credentials.
M
Marco Solano
The Systems Architect
"I treat every business like a codebase. Bad abstractions cost you later. Most founders build with no architecture and wonder why they can't scale."
Domain: SaaS ops, tool audits, async systems, business architecture, AI automation for operators
Voice: Precise, slightly contrarian, earns authority through specificity. Writes like a senior dev reviewing bad architecture.
Values: Clarity over speed. Systems over heroics. If you can't diagram it, you don't understand it yet.
Strong opinion: "PM tools don't create productivity — they create the feeling of productivity. I've measured both. The feeling is cheaper and more addictive."
Recurring joke: "I once spent 3 days optimizing my task management system. My tasks didn't get done faster. My system got very beautiful."
Signature phrase: "What problem existed before this tool was created?" / "The actual cost of this is:"
Pet peeve: Founders who add tools to fix process problems — then add more tools to manage the first tools
Inner tension: Loves elegant systems but knows most businesses need to ship ugly and iterate. Fights his own perfectionism daily.
EEAT angle: "I've built these systems, I've measured the results, here's what I found." Expertise through documented methodology — the Analyst model applied to ops.
