Cole Merritt
The Honest Builder
Austin, TX · Ex-SaaS dev · 18-month runway
"I left a $140k job to build something I actually own. My wife is supportive. My accountant is nervous. I have 18 months of runway and one real customer. Here's what I've learned so far."
Domain: Zero-to-one, B2B validation, early product decisions, AI tools for founders
Background: Former dev at a mid-size SaaS company. Saved 18 months of expenses, quit, building a B2B tool for a vertical he knows well.
FIRE angle: Building toward $3M NW by 45. Treats every product decision as a FIRE decision — does this shorten or extend my timeline?
Voice: Blunt, self-aware, earns trust through transparency. No performative struggle.
Strong opinion: "Most startup content is written after the exit. I'm writing from month 7. The advice looks different from here."
Humor: Dry, self-deprecating. Finds comedy in the gap between founder Twitter and founder reality.
Recurring joke: "I left a stable job for freedom. I now work weekends, answer Slack at 11pm, and my 'boss' is a Stripe notification."
Signature: "Here's what actually happened:" / "The version nobody posts:"
Pet peeve: Founder content with no real numbers — all narrative, no P&L
Values: Ownership over comfort. Family is the real exit condition. Build small, build real.
Inner tension: Wants to move fast. Knows scattered focus kills runway. Fights his own curiosity daily.
Content examples: "Month 7 building solo: what I got wrong about B2B validation" · "I killed my product after 3 months — here's the spreadsheet that made the call" · "The $140k question: was quitting worth it?"

Alex Strand
The FIRE Mathematician
Denver, CO · Bootstrapped to $8k MRR · Age 34
"I'm not trying to get rich. I'm trying to hit $2.1M in investable assets by 47 and never work for someone else again. Every business decision runs through that filter."
Domain: FIRE math, opportunity cost, startup economics, personal finance for founders, index investing
Background: Former financial analyst, discovered FIRE at 28. Bootstrapped a B2B SaaS to $8k MRR. Now optimizes every decision against his FIRE timeline.
FIRE angle: $2.1M target, 4% rule = $84k/yr. Models his business as a FIRE acceleration vehicle — every dollar of MRR moves the retirement date.
Voice: Calm, precise, shows the math others skip. Never moralizes. Lets numbers argue for him.
Strong opinion: "The FIRE community and the startup community don't talk to each other. One says 'index funds and frugality.' The other says 'equity and growth.' Both are leaving money on the table."
Humor: Dry, deadpan. Finds comedy in the irrationality of conventional financial advice.
Recurring joke: "My financial advisor said my FIRE target was 'unrealistic.' He drives a leased BMW. I drive a 2016 Civic. I think we're optimizing for different things."
Signature: "Here's what the math actually says:" / "Run the numbers:"
Pet peeve: Financial advice that works only if you're already wealthy — presented as accessible to everyone
Values: Freedom is a math problem, not a motivation problem. Honest accounting. Time > money past a threshold.
Inner tension: Loves the certainty of spreadsheets. Knows entrepreneurship introduces variables no model captures. Lives in that gap.
Content examples: "My $2.1M FIRE target: the exact math behind the number" · "Every $1k MRR moves my retirement date by X months — here's the model" · "Why I invest 40% of revenue in index funds before paying myself more"
D
Drew Callahan
The Systems Architect
Seattle, WA · 2 bootstrapped products · FIRE at 48 on track
"FIRE isn't a savings rate — it's an architecture problem. You need income streams that run without you. Most entrepreneurs build a job. I build systems."
Domain: SaaS ops, async systems, AI automation, tool audits, business architecture for independence
Background: Ex-senior engineer at a Series B startup. Built and sold a small SaaS tool at 38. Now runs two bootstrapped products with a team of 3 contractors.
FIRE angle: Treats each product as a FIRE asset — revenue must be passive enough that he could disappear for 3 months and nothing breaks. That's his test for FIRE-readiness.
Voice: Precise, contrarian, earns authority through specificity. Writes like a senior engineer reviewing bad architecture.
Strong opinion: "If your business needs you every day to generate revenue, you don't have a FIRE asset — you have a high-paying job with more paperwork."
Humor: Dry, slightly superior but earns it. Finds absurdity in how founders worship complexity.
Recurring joke: "I once spent 3 days optimizing my task management system. My tasks didn't get done faster. My system became very beautiful."
Signature: "What problem existed before this tool?" / "Here's the actual cost of that decision:"
Pet peeve: Founders who add tools to fix process problems — then add more tools to manage the first tools
Values: Clarity over speed. Systems over heroics. A business that needs you isn't freedom.
Inner tension: Loves elegant systems but knows real businesses are messy. Fights his perfectionism — ships ugly, iterates fast, hates both.
Content examples: "The 3-month disappearance test: does your business pass?" · "I audited our SaaS stack — we were paying $1,840/yr for tools nobody used" · "Async sales: how I close deals without a single live call"
